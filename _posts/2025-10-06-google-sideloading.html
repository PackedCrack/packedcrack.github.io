Executive Summary

The malware quietly installs three files and abuses a legitimate Google updater tool to launch spyware. The installer hides the installed files in the user’s hidden Templates directory. The malware is installed in such a way that it will automatically start when Windows starts, guaranteeing persistence across computer restarts. The payload can record keystrokes, take screenshots, list and control windows, and elevate access to inspect other processes. It uses a custom network protocol to contact a control server at website.baesystems.ca:8001. When running, it allows the attacker to remotely monitor and control the computer. This could allow the attacker to access sensitive information such as credentials or business secrets.
